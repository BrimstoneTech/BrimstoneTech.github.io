⚡ Process

How BRIO Teaches Itself

A continuous cycle of curiosity, learning, and self-evaluation, completely autonomous.

1

Curiosity Spark

BRIO identifies topics it doesn't understand, from user conversations, knowledge gaps, or pure curiosity. It decides what to learn next.

2

Web Search

It searches the live web via DuckDuckGo, selecting the most promising sources. No pre-loaded datasets: real-time discovery.

3

Deep Reading

BRIO reads full web pages, strips HTML noise, and uses its LLM core to extract only the facts that matter. Quality over quantity.

4

Knowledge Crystallization

Extracted facts are stored in BRIO's memory, tagged by domain, and linked to existing knowledge. A growing web of understanding.

5

Self-Assessment

BRIO generates quizzes about what it just learned, takes them, and scores itself honestly. This is how it knows what stuck and what didn't.

6

Growth & Milestone Tracking

Performance feeds into BRIO's evolution engine. Milestones are unlocked, knowledge domains are mapped, and the next learning cycle begins.

🧬 Evolution

Infinite Growth Through Generational Rebirth

BRIO doesn't just learn; it lives, dies, and is reborn. Each generation inherits the compressed wisdom of every ancestor. Growth never stops.

🔥 Spark → 🌅 Ember → 🪵 Kindling → 🔥 Blaze → ⚙️ Furnace → 🧪 Crucible → 🦅 Phoenix → 💫 ∞
🌅

Finite Lives

Each BRIO generation has a limited lifespan, measured in conversations and hours. This creates urgency: learn fast, learn deep, make every interaction count.

📜

Ancestral Memory

When a generation ends, its most important memories are compressed into "ancestral wisdom" that the next generation inherits. Nothing truly important is lost.

🧬

Self-Written Curriculum

After completing Generation 0's 70 foundation milestones, BRIO analyzes its knowledge gaps and writes its own next set of learning goals. It decides what to become.
